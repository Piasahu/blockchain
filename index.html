Decentralized Exchanges (DEXs)

How trustless trading works — from Automated Market Makers to on-chain order books and aggregators.

What are DEXs?

Decentralized Exchanges allow users to trade cryptocurrencies directly from their wallets without a centralized intermediary. Unlike Coinbase or Binance, DEXs use smart contracts to execute trades, providing trustless, permissionless, and non-custodial trading.

The two primary models are Automated Market Makers (AMMs), which use liquidity pools and mathematical formulas to determine prices, and Order Books, which match buyers and sellers like traditional exchanges but operate on-chain.

Constant Product Formula — the invariant that governs AMM pricing. As supply of token x increases, supply of token y decreases proportionally.

AMM Swap — Solidity 
// Constant Product AMM
// x * y = k (invariant)
function getAmountOut(
    uint amountIn,
    uint reserveIn,
    uint reserveOut
) public pure returns (uint amountOut) {
    uint amountInWithFee = amountIn * 997;
    uint numerator = amountInWithFee * reserveOut;
    uint denominator = (reserveIn * 1000) + amountInWithFee;
    amountOut = numerator / denominator;
}

Impermanent Loss (IL)

Impermanent Loss occurs when the price of tokens in a liquidity pool diverges from their price at deposit time. The loss is "impermanent" because it reverses if prices return to original ratios.

IL = 2√(p) / (1 + p) - 1

where p = price ratio change. At 2x price change, IL ≈ 5.7%. At 5x, IL ≈ 25.5%.

Liquidity providers earn trading fees to offset IL. For volatile pairs, IL can exceed fee income.

Key Innovations

◆ Singleton Architecture

All pools live in a single smart contract, reducing deployment costs by 99%. Gas savings for multi-hop swaps are significant since tokens stay in the same contract.

◆ Flash Accounting

Token transfers only settle on net balances at the end of a transaction. Intermediate operations don't require actual token movement, reducing gas costs dramatically.

What are Real World Assets (RWAs)?

Real World Assets (RWAs) are traditional financial assets — bonds, equities, real estate, commodities — tokenized on blockchain. By representing ownership as on-chain tokens, RWAs unlock 24/7 trading, fractional ownership, instant settlement, and global access.

The RWA market has reached $23B+ with 260% growth in 2025. Analysts expect it to 4x in 2026, and BCG forecasts $16.1T by 2030.

Resources & Glossary

Quick reference for blockchain and DeFi terminology, plus external resources for deeper learning.

Glossary

AMM
Automated Market Maker. A protocol that uses mathematical formulas and liquidity pools to enable decentralized token swaps.
TVL
Total Value Locked. The total value of crypto assets deposited in a DeFi protocol's smart contracts.
Flash Loan
An uncollateralized loan that must be borrowed and repaid within a single blockchain transaction.
Impermanent Loss
The temporary loss of value that liquidity providers experience when the price ratio of pooled assets changes.
Liquidity Pool
A smart contract holding paired tokens that enables decentralized trading. Providers earn fees.
Yield Farming
Earning returns by providing liquidity or staking tokens in DeFi protocols. Often involves multiple strategies.
Smart Contract
Self-executing code on a blockchain that automatically enforces the terms of an agreement.
Token
A digital asset on a blockchain. Can represent currency (ERC-20), collectibles (ERC-721), or governance rights.
NFT
Non-Fungible Token. A unique, indivisible digital asset on a blockchain, often used for art, identity, or deeds.
DAO
Decentralized Autonomous Organization. A governance structure where token holders vote on protocol decisions.
MEV
Maximal Extractable Value. Profit validators can extract by reordering, inserting, or censoring transactions.
Oracle
A service that provides off-chain data to smart contracts. Chainlink is the dominant oracle network.
RWA
Real World Assets. Physical assets (bonds, real estate, commodities) tokenized and brought on-chain.
Stablecoin
A token pegged to a stable asset (usually USD). Types: fiat-backed (USDC), algorithmic (GHO), over-collateralized (DAI).
Gas
The fee paid to validators for processing transactions on a blockchain. Measured in gwei on Ethereum.
Hooks (Uniswap)
Smart contract plugins that customize pool behavior at specific lifecycle points (before/after swap, etc.).
